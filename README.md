# IMGD5010_CreativeCoders
An assignment intended to expose others to an array of artists, and to encourage thinking critically about the art and tools they create.

## Matt Parker
### introduction to artist

Prof. Roberts and I spoke briefly about "stand-up comedian, #1-best-selling maths author and person who makes videos for the internet," British mathematician [Matt Parker](https://standupmaths.com/), whose creative work with Excel spreadsheets in particular has served as an inspiration for me. While this may not be sufficient to cover the requirements of the assignment, below please find links to Parker's work that may be of use or interest.

### video highlights

[Standup comedy routine about Spreadsheets](https://youtu.be/UBX2QQHlQ_I?si=OBQO9uIA6T3xxGyq))
My introduction to Parker; he demonstrates how an Excel spreadsheet can use conditional formatting to turn into a photograph. He also does so with charm and humor, modeling how to communicate otherwise dry subjects in an entertaining and informative manner.

[Why are Late Night Hosts So Bad at YouTube? A Closer Look](https://youtu.be/tEC8q9i2fOw?si=hnbSNjWWIm8kcbu3)
While not coding related, Parker hit more mainstream American audiences by providing an instructional video for late-night hosts (and others) during the COVID lockdown--and ended up being contacted as a resource by [Seth Meyers of Late Night](https://www.tiktok.com/@latenightseth/video/7337037040967519518).

[Someone improved my code by 40,832,277,770%](https://youtu.be/c33AZBnRHks?si=Q-hNfetAlWyWgfxi)
A portion of this video ended up in Parker's most recent "Getting Triggy with It" show, which I was fortunate enough to see at Edinburgh Fringe in 2025. The video itself is about, in some ways, a creative _way_ of approaching coding, but Parker's live show turned this incident into a livecoding performance (Python on a big screen!). The Edinburgh Review's [review of the show](https://edinburgh-reviews.co.uk/review-getting-triggy-with-it-matt-parker-does-the-maths-fringe-2025/) gave this glowing description:

_It must be said that a show featuring live Python code on a big screen could be a recipe for a very dry hour. Yet, in Parker’s hands, it’s utterly compelling. He uses the screen heavily, walking us through board game tiles and lines of code, and it’s a testament to his skill as a performer that every second is fascinating._
